# Social Psychology

Social norms: general guidelines for how we're supposed to behave in social contexts

   Reinforce "appropriate" behavior but punish "unacceptable" behavior

Social roles: behaviors associated with a position in a group

   Roles that we take on and act in a certain way to embody this role

Social scripts: culturally provided mental files for how to act in certain situations

Social psychology- how individuals perceive and think of other people, how the presence of others can influence our behaviors, the "Where we are (and who we're with) VS Who we are"

Social Cognition: process of thinking about ourselves and others in social context

   Dual Process Model of Social Cognition:

   1st Intuitive thought- a quick and effortless, fairly automatic thought

   2nd Deliberative thought- a careful and effortful, slower, rational thought, systematical reasoning

1st impressions are guided by our schemas related to others (knowledge and expectations about individuals/groups)

   In research using 6 second snippets of lectures, participants can form *reliable* judgments very quickly and with very little information (trustworthiness, competence, likability, and aggressiveness) and found that more information didn't really improve accuracy just increased confidence in their assessments

1st impressions produce quick and long lasting effects on how we perceive and behave toward others, however these impressions can also affect how we behavior and normally make our first impression come true

Attributions: our explanations for why people behave the way they do, the "why" matter more than the "what", and we normally have a self-serving bias (believing or pushing blame to external factors)

   Internal attributions: dispositional, our intelligence, gender, ethnicity, and personality, and emotions. Normally are consistent (besides emotions)

   External attributions: time of day, surrounding environment, experiences with others

Attribution errors-

Fundamental attribution error- tendency to overestimate dispositional (internal, stable) causes of behaviors and to underestimate external causes of behaviors

   Example: Bobby hit Jimmy-

Ultimate attribution error- the tendency to ascribe the cause of a behavior to dispositional characteristics of the **group** rather than to an individual member

   Example: Stereotypes (Old people are bad at driving so it makes sense that an old person will cut me off when driving)

Self-serving bias: we attribute causes for our own behavior in a way that is best for our self-esteem

   When something good happens it's because of our internal attribution, when something bad happens it's because of an external attribution

   In a clinical context, depressed patients or those with anxiety show the opposite pattern believing that when something bad happens they deserved it and when something good happens they brush it off or blame external attributes.

Cognitive dissonance: psychological tension caused when an individual has a thought and a behavior that are inconsistent with one another; experiences motivation to reduce this discrepancy

   When two thoughts don't make sense to us and we try to make sense of them together

   When we see dissonance, we change attitude, behavior, or rationalize behavior

   Example:

   Attitude/Belief: "I'm on a diet so I'm avoiding all fatty food"

   Action: Someone brought in donuts

      Change attitude/belief- "I'm allowed to cheat sometimes"/"I'll start tomorrow"

      Change action- Not eating the donuts

      Rationalize- "I'll spend an extra 30 minutes at the gym"

   Mrs. Keech and the Seekers

      When people thought that there was going to be a Great Flood on 12/21/54 and that Aliens from Clarion will send rescue spaceships to save them.

      When the 21st came and it didn't happen, they rationalized that it would still happen but happen on the 24th instead. When it didn't happen again, they rationalized this time that they "saved the world" through the power of their prayer because God was moved by the amount of religious strength they showed.

Social Facilitation: when an individual's performance is better in the presence of others compared to alone in more simple tasks/routine tasks (running/cycling) but when an individual's performance is worse in the presence of others compared to alone in complex tasks (like a more complex maze).

   Social facilitation and arousal- the presence of others increase our level of arousal, arousal facilitates easy tasks but it impairs difficult tasks

Social loafing: when an individual working as part of a group reduces their effort, when we think someone else will already do what we were tasked to do/would do or when we think our effort is insignificant

Mimicry (Chameleon effect): copying behavior of others, an unconscious behavior when we change behavior to fit one person around us. We usually have positive feelings towards people that mimic us as well

Conformity: When we change behavior to fit in with a group of people, is normally a much more conscious behavior as well

Groupthink: when group members try to avoid conflicts and strive for agreement despite not actually agreeing or being confident with the "solution".

Stereotypes: set of simplistic beliefs (schema) about a group of people that is activated by some sign of group membership (clothing, skin color, etc)

Prejudice and discrimination: preconceived attitude (prejudice) based on stereotypes and resulting negative behavior (discrimination) toward members of the group

   beliefs- stereotype

   attitude/feeling- prejudice

   behavior- discrimination

Micro-aggression- small slight or offense that may be intentional but normally unintentional and does not harm the target of the offenses in any major way but can accumulate to be burdensome over time

   micro-assault- blatant verbal, nonverbal, or environmental attack intentionally discriminatory or biases

   micro-insult- unintentional behavior or verbal comment conveying rudeness or insensitivity

   micro-invalidation- an action that excludes, negates, or dismisses the perceptions of the target

Ingroup Bias: tendency to favor individuals from within our group over those from outside our group

Outgroup homogeneity: tendency to view all individuals outside our group as highly similar

Psychoanalytic-

Dream analysis, free association, rorschach inkblots, thematic apperception(tat)

      Ego: conscious part that balances the Id and Superego, based on reality

      Superego: "conscience" moral compass

   Defense Mechanism:

         Repression- unconscious mechanism employed by the ego to keep disturbing or threatening thoughts from becoming conscious

         Denial: blocking external events from awareness

         Projection: attributing their own feelings and thoughts to another person

         Displacement: taking an impulse (aggression) to a substitute object

         Regression: movement back in psychological time when one is face with them

         Sublimation: satisfying impulse with substitute object but in a socaily acceptable way
