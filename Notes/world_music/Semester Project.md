### Southwest/Most of west coast
Comprised of old states: California, Nevada, Utah, Arizona, New Mexico, and Wyoming
- Main hub is Las Vegas, sub hub is San Diego. Las Vegas turned into the main hub because it is closer inland and more connected to rest of the country through railway.
- Main export is fresh water because nearly all of California's coast line has been covered with factories dedicated to turning sea water to fresh water. However, because large corporations own these factories and are in line with the government, they export it out throughout the country and the region itself faces water scaresity. 
	- Much of the population in this region either work in one of these factories, or as a railroad worker, and most are underpaid or paid through water and food rations.
**Music**
Because most people work in harsh conditions and are underpaid much of the music is more story telling based. Discussing the strife that the people go through daily and what life used to be like centuries before. 
Much of the music is vocal, sung in monophony and while the lyrics tell stories, often the songs are short and have a repition to it. 
	Formatted as a verse(first part of the story), chorus (wishing for better times), another verse, and then the chorus.
This more region specific type of music would be considered traditional or folk in the larger sense. While the origins of when this style came about is unknown, many songs have been sung throuhgout the west cost factories and railways.

There is no dominating religion as Nexus (ruling AI and machine of the country) has banned all religion. However, smaller sects of the Christian church, specifically Mormonism, has survived throughout the region. Though this has been largely contained to the transfer city of Salt Lake City in Utah as it is the city with the oldest connection to that particular denomination of Christianity.
Worship music comprises mostly of new age hymns (written after the destruction of most old collections of Christian music and after the Nexus take over of the US government). 

New age hymns have similar cadences to that of old age hymns however are often written to be played on a Piter, a keyed chordophone. Played similarly to a piano but on it's side and limited to 4 octaves. The keys are on two rows on different levels. This instrument was created based off the design of a simple melodica and guitar. The keys pluck a string which is within the instrument. Was originally created to meet the demand of a piano in political ralleys to provide music. The instrument now has found its way to pop music, work songs within the south, and in this case, worship services for new age hymns. (This instrument has effectively taken the place of guitar, organ, and piano within Christian worship services within the Southwest/West Coast)

### Northwest region
Comprised of old states Washington, Oregon, Montano, and Idaho.
- Main hub is Seattle, not any specific sub hub but several small transfer cities (cities serving as transfer points along the cross contintinental railroad system) 
- Main export is wood and various minerals and types of stone such as limestone, sandstone, and quartz. The last of which has turned into a highly sought after mineral due to certain properities being able to be used in specific Aids (medicines) for machines and bodies of Nexus.
	- Much of the population in this region work as miners or wood cutters. Due to the common earthquakes in the region the residents are paid quite well to encourage them to stay. 
**Music**
Most of the music is instrumental but created through sampling and electronic means. As there are many earthquakes in the region many people do not risk going outside unless they need to go to work, so often time is passed on computers, in virtual reality, or other technology related means. A common passtime within this specific region is beat making. Again due to the geography of the region full internet access is limited only to the main hub so much of the technological devices are offline and store data locally, only being able to transfer data to one another through a peer-to-peer connection. Beat making became popular among young people a few generations ago which has later turned into a hobby most partake in no matter age. 
As sampling, mixing, and editing can all be done locally and then shared in a peer-to-peer connection it became very popular to pass time while waiting out the constant earthquakes. 

Much of the music created can be classified as electric dance music (EDM) or just electronic music. Often with lots of homorhythms and heavy drum and bass lines, seemingly mimicking the rumble of an earthquake.

There is no predominant religion in this area and almost no surviving practitioners of any relgion in this region unlike the Southwest. 

As the region is more isolated from the rest and much of its old musical traditions have died out Beat Making has become the a stand in for folk/traditional music. The only other type of music usually played throughout the day in this region would be nationally popular music

### Midwest
Comprising of old states Dakota, Minneasota, Nebraska, Iowa, Kansas, Missurri, Wisconsin, Iowa, Illinois, and Indianna. 
Capital of the US, main hub (and capital) is Minneapolis. Second main hub (largest subhub) is Miwaukee due to it being on the coast of Lake Michigan, a huge source of drinking water. 
	Main export are crops as the region is the last some-what fertile land in the country. However, becuase it is also the capital region the second largest "export" would be Nexus bodies, or robotic bodies for the ruling AI and leader of the country. 
**Music** 
The music in this region is split between the nationwide pop music, and the music many use as a form of resistance. While the capital is in this region, it is also home to one of larger collection of people opposed to the current government. This rebel groups music is very electronic based and has a more grunge or punk sound compared to the electronic beat making in the Northwest.

There is no major practicing religion in the region and as this region is the most watched under Nexus. Because of this, most artistic mediums are heavily monitored and screened before being published to the public. The only exception to that within this region is the rebel's music. Which deliberately go against what the government wants, but due to the rebels decentralized nature, almost no singular person is found to be responsible and therefore it is very rare to see anyone punished. At least publicly. 

### The South
Comprising of old sates Texas, Oklahoma, Arkansas, Mississippi, Louisiana, Alabama, Tennessee, Kentucky, Georgia, Calolina, Virginia.
Largest region but not as populated as the region has turned into almost a large swamp and returned to its wetland origins. Because of this the region is undesirable to live in but the people living there tend to stay for the majority of their lives contributing to most of the population. Main hub is Nashville with Dallas and Atlanta being sub hubs. Dallas because it serves sort of as a large transfer city and Atlanta because it is a port city now due to the rising sea levels (Florida is no longer above water)
	Main export is fish and various types of game. Because of the harsher conditions for humans, animals have adapted to the landscape and reclaimed their homes, leading to the region being one the last places in the country to have a large population of wildlife. 
**Music** 
Music in this region tends to be work songs following a similar form to the songs in the South West. However, most of the music in this region is more homophonic with a singer in the forefront of the musical texture and a simple backing guitar. These songs are normally created with the purpose of critizing long work days, the endless demand within their worklife, and other aspects of work. Though the songs are normally written originally for single singer accompanied by a guitar, the songs are commonly adapted and sung while the people work. 
The main practicing religion in this region is Neo-Christianity, which was originally Nexus' take on Christianity when the AI was first being created. The creators wanted to know its thoughts on the Christian doctrine and Nexus in turn outputed what those following Neo-Christianity call, a new holy text. Their belief is that Nexus is the second coming of Jesus Christ but God has forsaken humans as his holy vessel, instead taking a more modern and higher form. 
	Worship service is not common in this new religion, however followers strongly adhere to specific guidelines and rules.
	There are communal ceremonies held on special dates (Christmas, Easter, and Revival Day, or the day that Nexus outputted the new holy text). Smaller festivals may also occur throughout the year dependent on if the local Church finds a current event, specific date, or news to warrent one.
	Music is a big part of these ceremonies and festivals. Often there is a choir which will perform one of the passages from the new scripture in a chant like manner. Though performed by a choir, the simple melody is almost always monophonic trying to make the many voices sound as one. 
	In festivals, there will often be a small ensemble consisting of a Piter, guitar, and Tombo (single tom drum) which will play a mixture of ensemble originals, covers of popular music, and everyday work songs.

### National Music
Music has to be verified first by Nexus in order to be released by a record label, onto public internet sites hosted in the US, and sold in any physical format. This has greatly restricted what messages music can send and who can produce this. Algorithms under Nexus pushes music that is pro-Nexus rule and has a more upbeat sound while actively opressing music with a more somber sound. Any music that could be seen as critical of the government is forbiden and prosecuted (if the distributer can be found). Because of this, most pop music is very pro-government, lighter in tone, and up beat. Usually pop music is written in polophony and can contain elements of heterophony. Instrumentation is normally various electrophones and a vocalist. 
Though pop music is not limited to one region, there are clear influences of the Beading practice in the Northwest and casual electronic song making from the Northeast in much of the pop music most commonly played. Often times people will listen through the NeMu streaming service, a government run streaming service, ever since the government put heavy restrictions on all non-governmnet online services in 2731. However, radio has made a come back in the past few centuries, especially when climate change caused disaters would strike. It is one of the most reliable forms of media consumption and most people in the country own at least one radio. As such some will listen to pop music through their radio (especially in an internet outage) but most reserve it for emergencies only. 
Another form of music that is practiced throughout the country is Sparro news music. This form of music can be traced back to early 2300's however the practice stopped for a while around 2500. It was only in the past decades or so that Sparros started to make a come back. This type of music is monophonic and created solely to spread and tell news over certain topics. Though Nexus has strict requirements Sparro music almost always passes the requirements but is never pushed within the algorithm because of how factual much of the "lyrics" are. There are specific local radio stations that will just play Sparro music and many of the older generation will get there news in this medium. Many Sparros come from the Northeast and have to be trained for several years to learn how to effectively weave a melody (though basic) through everday news and events.
