Musical Traits:
-   Often monophonic and vocal
-   Instruments, if present, are generally unpatched percussion
-   Vocal style is generally tense with pulsations on long notes
-   Songs possess supernatural power
-   Song is not composed but “given” in visions or dreams
-   Technical complexity less important than cultural or spiritual power of a song
-   Most songs are preserved through oral tradition
-   Songs are associated with specific events, objects, ceremonies, and activities

Kachinas
	-   Religious icons associated with Pueblo Tribes (Hopi and Zuni)
	-   Representation of figures or concepts from nature
	-   White bear
	-   Mudhead (clown)
	-   Owl 
	-   Sun

Plains: Powwow Song, "Aapiichii Daaniigamowaa" 
	- communal singing
	- leaders then response then mix together
	- drums and shaker type in background
	- drums lead and set beat
	- seems to be majority men singing (at least in the forefront)
Common Plains Song form: ||: A A(') BC  BC :||  a divided or incomplete 

Eastern Woodland: Creek Stomp Dance Song
	- much more direct call response almost round like
	- stomping to set beat
	- balanced call and response (solo caller, one-three or so people to respond) 
	- non-instrumental

Great Basin: Pawnee Ghost Dance Song ("The Yellow Star")
	- small wood block almost thing in the background
	- single singer
	- very rhythmic vocal line

Yuman: Walapai Funeral Song
	- some light percussion in the background can't tell
	- mostly vocal 
	- very repetitive
	- monophonic but a couple people singing

Arctic: Inuit Katajjaq Singing Game
	- more of a game, the performers are the audience 
	- uses throat singing (singing a in more growling way in the throat and then having an overtone)
	- the "winner" of the game is the one who can go the longest without running out of breath or laughing

#### Powwows and Pan-Amerindian Identity
Purpose of powwows:
	- Celebrations of Amerindian Identity and intertribal unity
	- Public competitions of singing and dancing
		- Many categories of dance (hoop, shawl, fancy, grass, war, etc)
Common body of songs exist for powwows 
	- based on Plains style
Non-Amerindians may attend or participate in some events ("intertribal dances")
	- generally not allowed to take part in competitive events
	- normally music and communnity dance are the only ways to interact directly with the tribes

**Other intertribal musics**
Native American Church (NAC)
	- Hybrid of Christianity and peyote worship
		- Peyote: small catus; source of the hallucinogen mescaline
		- "buttons" are dried and eaten, smoked, or mixed with liquid
	- Ceremonies also include many prayers, songs, and ritual fires
		- songs are normally derived from Navajo style
		- may be accompanied by water-filled drums or rattles
		- features distinctive vocalbles (syllabus that don't have syntactical meaning)
The Ghost Dance (an outlawed practice that is no longer practiced due various reasons)
	- Messianic cult of the Siouz and Arapaho founded by Wovoka, spread to Plains tribes
	- Main point were getting rid of and defeating the whites, bring back the buffalo, and resurrect the dead
	- Contravened official US policy of Christianziation of the natives; led to massacre at Wounded Knee (1890)
